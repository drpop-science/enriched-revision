Q7(a)(i) bState the name of one quaternary consumer.[1]
💡 Walkthrough
1
A quaternary consumer is the 4th consumer in a chain. Count from the producer.
2
Chain 1: corn → grasshopper (1st) → rat (2nd) → snake (3rd) → eagle (4th) ✓
3
Chain 2: corn → grasshopper (1st) → frog (2nd) → snake (3rd+) — snake is also a quaternary consumer in some chains in this web.
4
CAIE accepts: eagle OR snake as quaternary consumer.
✅ Mark Scheme
✓eagle [1] OR snake [1] (either is acceptable — check against the food web)
⚠️ Common Mistakes
✗Only writing 'eagle' — both eagle and snake can be quaternary consumers in this web.
Q7(a)(i) cState the name of one organism at the first trophic level.[1]
💡 Walkthrough
1
Trophic level 1 = producers. These make their own food by photosynthesis and are always at the start of a food chain.
2
In this food web: corn and mango are both producers — either is correct.
✅ Mark Scheme
✓corn [1] OR mango [1] (any producer from the diagram)
⚠️ Common Mistakes
✗Writing 'grasshopper' — it eats corn so it is at trophic level 2 (primary consumer).
Q7(a)(ii)State three ways that energy is lost between trophic levels.
Write each way on a separate numbered line: 1.   2.   3.[3]
💡 Walkthrough
1
Respiration / heat / metabolic processes — organisms use energy for movement, growth and maintaining body temperature. This energy escapes as heat and cannot be passed on.
2
Not all of the organism is eaten — predators often leave parts of their prey (bones, roots, shells). That energy stays in those uneaten parts.
3
Not all the organism is digested / egestion / faeces / urine — undigested material is egested as faeces; metabolic waste leaves as urine. Neither returns to the food chain.
4
Other accepted answers: movement as a separate point; synthesis of biological molecules / growth (energy stored in new tissues not passed on); any other valid physiological process (AVP).
✅ Mark Scheme
✓respiration / heat / metabolic processes [1]
✓not all of the organism is eaten [1]
✓not all the organism is digested / egestion / faeces / urine [1]
✓movement [1] OR synthesis of biological molecules / growth [1] (any 3 from the list above)
⚠️ Common Mistakes
✗Writing 'energy is lost' with no specific mechanism — always name HOW it is lost.
✗Listing the same idea twice in different words (e.g. egestion AND faeces) — CAIE counts these as one point.
Q7(a)(iii)State the principal source of energy for all biological systems.[1]
💡 Walkthrough
1
All food chains trace back to light energy from the Sun.
2
Plants capture this light via photosynthesis — but the original source is the Sun itself.
3
⚠️ 'Photosynthesis' = the process that uses the energy. 'Glucose' = how the energy is stored. Neither is the source.
✅ Mark Scheme
✓Sun [1] (Do NOT accept 'photosynthesis' or 'glucose')
⚠️ Common Mistakes
✗Writing 'photosynthesis' — that is the process, not the energy source.

Deforestation removes trees — this destroys the habitat (home, nesting sites, shelter) of animals that lived there.
2

3

Energy Efficiency — Trophic Level Calculations
IGCSE Biology 0610 · 19.2.17 & 19.2.18 · Supplement
🧮 Formula to memorise:
Efficiency (%) = (Energy at higher trophic level ÷ Energy at lower trophic level) × 100
Percentage lost (%) = 100 − efficiency (%)
CONCEPTWhy does energy decrease at each trophic level? (maize food chain)
🦅 Eagle — 10 kJ
🐍 Snake — 100 kJ
🦎 Lizard — 1 000 kJ
🦗 Locust — 10 000 kJ
🌽 Maize — 100 000 kJ
💡Only about 10% of energy passes from one level to the next. The rest (~90%) is lost as heat (respiration), egestion (faeces), uneaten parts, movement, and growth.
EC1The food chain shows: maize (100 000 kJ) → locusts (10 000 kJ) → lizards (1 000 kJ) → snakes (100 kJ) → eagle (10 kJ).
Calculate the efficiency of energy transfer from locusts to lizards. Show your working. [2][2]
💡 Walkthrough
1
Identify the two levels: energy at lizards = 1 000 kJ. Energy at locusts = 10 000 kJ.
2
Apply formula: efficiency = (1 000 ÷ 10 000) × 100 = 10%
✅ Mark Scheme
✓correct working shown [1]
✓= 10% [1]
EC2Using the same food chain, calculate the percentage of energy lost between maize and lizards. [2][2]
💡 Walkthrough
1
Energy at maize = 100 000 kJ. Energy at lizards = 1 000 kJ.
2
Efficiency = (1 000 ÷ 100 000) × 100 = 1%
3
% lost = 100 − 1 = 99%
✅ Mark Scheme
✓efficiency = 1% [1]
✓% lost = 99% [1]
⚠️ Common Mistakes
✗Forgetting to subtract from 100 — the question asks for % LOST, not % transferred.
EC3A grassland food chain: grass (50 000 kJ) → rabbits (5 000 kJ) → foxes (400 kJ).
(a) Efficiency: grass → rabbits?   (b) Efficiency: rabbits → foxes?   (c) Which transfer is less efficient? Suggest one reason. [3][3]
💡 Walkthrough
1
(a) Grass → rabbits: (5 000 ÷ 50 000) × 100 = 10%
2
(b) Rabbits → foxes: (400 ÷ 5 000) × 100 = 8%
3
(c) Rabbits → foxes is less efficient. Possible reason: foxes are warm-blooded and lose more energy as heat to maintain body temperature; OR foxes leave more uneaten parts.
✅ Mark Scheme
✓(a) 10% [1]
✓(b) 8% [1]
✓(c) rabbits → foxes is less efficient; valid reason e.g. more heat loss [1]
EC4Energy at the tertiary consumer level = 20 kJ. Assuming 10% efficiency at each transfer, calculate the energy available at the producer level. Show all working. [2][2]
💡 Walkthrough
1
Work backwards — if 10% passes up, multiply by 10 going back each level.
2
Tertiary consumer (20 kJ) → secondary consumer (20 ÷ 0.1 = 200 kJ) → primary consumer (2 000 kJ) → producer (20 000 kJ)
✅ Mark Scheme
✓correct method shown [1]
✓20 000 kJ [1]
⚠️ Common Mistakes
✗Multiplying instead of dividing — if 10% is transferred UP, the level BELOW must have 10× more, so divide going down.
EC5Maize chain: maize (10 000 kJ) → locusts (1 000 kJ) → lizards (100 kJ) → snakes (10 kJ).
(a) Efficiency: maize → locusts?   (b) Efficiency: maize → snakes?   (c) Why is energy in snakes much less than maize? [3][3]
💡 Walkthrough
1
(a) Maize → locusts: (1 000 ÷ 10 000) × 100 = 10%
2
(b) Maize → snakes: (10 ÷ 10 000) × 100 = 0.1%
3
(c) Energy is lost at each trophic level — as heat in respiration, in egestion and in uneaten parts. Very little remains by trophic level 4.
✅ Mark Scheme
✓(a) 10% [1]
✓(b) 0.1% [1]
✓(c) energy lost at each trophic level; e.g. heat from respiration / egestion [1]

⚠️ Easy to Confuse
VS
Egestion — undigested food as faeces · 排粪 · การขับถ่ายกาก
Excretion — metabolic waste (urea, CO₂) · 排泄 · การขับถ่ายของเสีย
VS

Respiration — releases CO₂ into air; occurs in mitochondria · 呼吸作用 · การหายใจ
VS

Decomposition — dead organisms → CO₂ (via bacteria/fungi respiration) · 分解 · การย่อยสลาย
